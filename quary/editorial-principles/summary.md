## Summary

- Extends the title — adds the dimension compression had to drop.
- Stands alone — a reader scanning summaries gets the gist without opening the file.
- Front-loads — the most informative phrase first; one sentence delivers the bottom line.
- Bridges to the body — telegraphs what the body proves so the reader can choose to read on.
- Stays in the title's voice — declarative, present-tense, specific.

A summary sits between title and body, physically (line 3) and figuratively (title compresses, summary unfolds, body proves).

One to three sentences on the line after the H1, with a blank line between.
