# Expository ordering

Main idea before mechanics; conclusions before justifications. The reader who stops after the first paragraph still leaves with the right impression.

## How to apply

- Open with a lede — one or two sentences naming what the document is for and what the reader gets from reading it.
- Usage before internals. Public surface before architecture. How to do it before how it works.
- Conclusions before justifications. State the position, then explain.

## Why

The reader's attention is most concentrated at the start. The most important information lands when the reader is most ready to receive it. Burying the conclusion behind a wall of buildup forces the reader to wade for it.
