# Oxford comma

Use the Oxford comma in lists of three or more — `nodes, edges, and memos`.

## How to apply

- Three or more items: comma after each item, including before the final conjunction.
- Two items: no comma before the conjunction (`nodes and edges`).
- The Oxford comma disambiguates compound items in long lists; without it, ambiguity creeps in.

## Why

`I'd like to thank my parents, Ayn Rand and God` reads as "my parents = Ayn Rand and God." With the Oxford comma: `I'd like to thank my parents, Ayn Rand, and God` — three distinct entities.
