## Title

- Identifies distinctively — a title grep returns the right artifact.
- Promises the content — title is what the artifact is, not what it is about.
- Compresses to memorable shape — short, citable, holds in the head.
- Hooks recognition — the reader knows which artifact to open.
- Sets the artifact's voice — declarative, present-tense, specific.

A title names without exhausting — substance is for the summary and body. Title takes whatever shape the content demands — claim, decision, question, topic, reference.

Concrete over abstract. Use the user's title when given; otherwise derive from topic, content, conversation.
