# Anticipate the reader's question

Write what the reader needs to know next, not what's next in the writer's head.

## How to apply

- After each paragraph, ask: "what would the reader want to know next?"
- Answer that question in the next paragraph — even if it isn't where your outline goes.
- The writer's order of discovery is rarely the reader's order of need. Reorder for the reader.

## Common failures

- Walking through every step of the discovery process before naming what was discovered.
- Answering an unasked meta-question ("you might be wondering why...") instead of the question the reader actually has.
- Putting the answer at the end, where only readers who finish find it.
