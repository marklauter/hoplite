# Within-document cohesion read

Read paragraph transitions. Does each pick up from the previous, or do paragraphs sit as independent claims?

## How to apply

- Read consecutive paragraphs in order.
- After each transition, check: did the second paragraph's opening pick up from the first paragraph's close? Or did it jump?
- If it jumped, either add connecting tissue (reference chain, transitional clause, theme/rheme continuity) or recognize that the paragraphs are a hidden list and convert them to bullets.

## What cohesion looks like

- Topic continuity: paragraph N+1 opens with a noun phrase that picks up from paragraph N's close.
- Reference chains: pronouns, definite articles, and demonstratives point at antecedents established in the previous paragraph.
- Theme/rheme: each sentence starts with known information (the theme) and ends with new (the rheme); the next sentence picks up the rheme as its new theme.
